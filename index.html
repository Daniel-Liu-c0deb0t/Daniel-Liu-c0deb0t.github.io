/about/

I am a high school student at Torrey Pines High School that is interested in computer science.

Currently, I have two main research interests: applying string algorithms and data structures to bioinformatics problems, and evaluating the security and adversarial robustness of deep learning systems.

I also have other projects in machine learning, programming languages, and game development. I am generally interested in almost any computer science topic, and I enjoy solving problems related to computer science and algorithms. I find applying algorithms to real-life problems very interesting.

I blog about research and programming here.

_machine_learning_

  • 2019 Adversarial Point Perturbations on 3D Objects
    Contributions
    • Four new algorithms for attacking neural networks.
    • Three "shape attacks" that are realistic and robust against point removal defenses.
    • One distributional attack that maintains the shape of a 3D object.
    • Daniel Liu, Ronald Yu, Hao Su
    [arxiv] [github]
    • Preprint paper.
    • In collaboration with Ronald Yu and Hao Su, from the Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) department at University of California San Diego (UCSD).
  • 2019 Extending Adversarial Attacks and Defenses to Deep 3D Point Cloud Classifiers
    Contributions
    • New algorithms to attack 3D point clouds.
    • New methods to defend 3D point clouds against adversarial attacks.
    • Analysis of the behavior of 3D point cloud networks against adversarial attacks.
    • Daniel Liu, Ronald Yu, Hao Su
    [arxiv] [github]
    • Abridged version peer reviewed and accepted at the 2019 IEEE International Conference on Image Processing (ICIP). I was selected to receive a travel grant from the IEEE Signal Processing Society for the conference.
    • Defense technique presented in a talk by my advisor Hao Su at a workshop in the 2019 Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR).
    • Alternative version of the paper and the poster appeared in the Greater San Diego Science and Engineering Fair and the California Science and Engineering Fair.
    • In collaboration with Ronald Yu and Hao Su, from the Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) department at University of California San Diego (UCSD).

_bioinformatics_

  • 2019 Algorithms for efficiently collapsing reads with Unique Molecular Identifiers
    Contributions
    • New formulation of the Unique Molecular Identifier (UMI) deduplication problem that enables optimizations.
    • Novel data structures to efficiently search for similar UMIs.
    • New tool/library called UMICollapse.
    • Daniel Liu
    [biorxiv] [github]
    • Preprint paper, currently under review.
  • 2019 Fuzzysplit: demultiplexing and trimming sequenced DNA with a declarative language
    Contributions
    • New idea for matching patterns in sequenced DNA through the use of a domain-specific language.
    • Novel algorithms for parsing and matching user-specified patterns, which are implemented in Fuzzysplit.
    • Daniel Liu
    [peerj] [biorxiv] [github]
    • Peer reviewed and accepted at PeerJ - the Journal of Life and Environmental Sciences.
  • 2017 FastQParse: An Integrated Toolkit for the Genotyping by Sequencing Pipeline
    Contributions
    • Implementation of many different algorithms in a coherent toolkit to easily automate the preprocessing step for sequenced DNA.
    • Daniel Liu, JianJun Gao, Abraham Palmer
    [github]
    • Presented at the Research Experiences for (High School and) Undergraduates (REU/REHU) symposium at the University of Chicago. I was the only high school student.
    • Work done at an internship at Palmer Lab in University of California San Diego (UCSD).
