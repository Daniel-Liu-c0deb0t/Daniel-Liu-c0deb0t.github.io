about

I am a high school student at Torrey Pines High School that is interested in computer science. I will be a freshman at the University of California Los Angeles very soon!

I like to make my code go very, very fast. Anything from high-level algorithms to low-level micro-optimizations is fair game for me.

I support the open science and open source software movements. I would not have been able to do any projects without them.

I've had two main research interests so far: applying efficient string algorithms to bioinformatics problems, and evaluating the security of deep learning systems.

I've also done lots of programming projects in machine learning, programming languages, game development, etc. I like to reside in the niche between computer science theory and real-life practicality. I write Rust, Java, and Python with Vim on Linux.

I blog about research and programming here. If you are a high school student and you are wondering how to get into research, then I highly recommend checking out my post here.

papers

machine_learning

  • 2019 Adversarial Point Perturbations on 3D Objects
    Contributions
    • Four new algorithms for attacking neural networks.
    • Three "shape attacks" that are realistic and robust against point removal defenses.
    • One distributional attack that maintains the shape of a 3D object.
    • Daniel Liu, Ronald Yu (UCSD), Hao Su (UCSD)
    [arxiv] [github]
    • Preprint paper.
  • 2019 Extending Adversarial Attacks and Defenses to Deep 3D Point Cloud Classifiers
    Contributions
    • New algorithms to attack 3D point clouds.
    • New methods to defend 3D point clouds against adversarial attacks.
    • Analysis of the behavior of 3D point cloud networks against adversarial attacks.
    • Daniel Liu, Ronald Yu (UCSD), Hao Su (UCSD)
    [ieee xplore] [arxiv] [github]
    • Peer reviewed and accepted at the 2019 IEEE International Conference on Image Processing. I was selected to receive a travel grant from the IEEE Signal Processing Society for my oral presentation at the conference.

bioinformatics

  • 2019 Algorithms for efficiently collapsing reads with Unique Molecular Identifiers
    Contributions
    • New formulation of the Unique Molecular Identifier (UMI) deduplication problem that enables optimizations.
    • Novel data structures to efficiently search for similar UMIs.
    • New tool/library called UMICollapse.
    • Daniel Liu
    [peerj] [biorxiv] [github]
    • Peer reviewed and accepted at PeerJ - the Journal of Life and Environmental Sciences.
    • Invited to give talks to Pachter Lab at Caltech and Goodarzi Lab at UC San Francisco.
  • 2019 Fuzzysplit: demultiplexing and trimming sequenced DNA with a declarative language
    Contributions
    • New idea for matching patterns in sequenced DNA through the use of a domain-specific language.
    • Novel algorithms for parsing and matching user-specified patterns, which are implemented in a tool called Fuzzysplit.
    • Daniel Liu
    [peerj] [biorxiv] [github]
    • Peer reviewed and accepted at PeerJ - the Journal of Life and Environmental Sciences.
  • 2017 FastQParse: An Integrated Toolkit for the Genotyping by Sequencing Pipeline
    Contributions
    • Implementation of many different algorithms in a coherent toolkit called FastQParse to easily automate the preprocessing step for sequenced DNA.
    • Daniel Liu, JianJun Gao (UCSD), Abraham Palmer (UCSD)
    [github]
    • Presented at the Research Experiences for (High School and) Undergraduate Students symposium at the University of Chicago.
